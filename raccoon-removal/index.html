Nocturnal Noises in Attic or Chimney

Raccoons are primarily nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night. If you hear scratching, thumping, or vocalizations coming from your attic, chimney, or walls during the evening hours, it's a strong indicator that you might have a raccoon guest. These sounds can range from soft rustling to loud movements as the animal navigates your home's structure. Mother raccoons with kits can be particularly noisy. Pay attention to the timing and location of these sounds. While rodents can also be noisy, raccoon sounds are generally larger and more deliberate. Prompt investigation of unusual nighttime noises is recommended to determine the source. Don't dismiss persistent sounds within your home's structure. Animals nesting inside can cause acoustic disturbances that are hard to ignore, especially at night when everything else is quiet.

Damaged Garbage Cans and Spilled Contents

Raccoons are notorious for raiding garbage cans in search of food. If you consistently find your trash cans tipped over, lids removed, and contents scattered across your yard, raccoons are likely the culprits. Properly securing your trash can lids can help deter them, but once they've discovered a reliable food source, they will keep coming back. The presence of trash strewn around your property is a clear sign of nocturnal foraging activity. This is a common issue for people living in or near areas where raccoons frequent. Securing your garbage is a fundamental step in pest prevention. Even tightly sealed containers can be breached by determined raccoons.

Evidence of Digging and Foraging

Raccoons often dig in yards, gardens, and lawns in search of insects, grubs, and other food sources. They have sharp claws and a strong desire to find food, leading to noticeable damage to landscaping. This digging can damage your lawn and plantings.
